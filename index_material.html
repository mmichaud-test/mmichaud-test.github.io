Materials

Most materials are metals; they can be used to make metal weapons and armor. The GM is the final arbiter of what items can be made using a material. An item can be made with no more than one precious material, and only an expert in Crafting can create it. Some rare and exotic materials require master or even legendary proficiency.(For more on crafting precious materials, see GC 253.)

Precious Materials

Materials with the precious trait can be substituted for base materials. For example, a hammer’s head could be made of byeshk instead of iron. Items made of a precious material cost more than typical items; not only does precious material cost more, but the crafter must invest more time working with it. In addition, more powerful items require precious materials of greater purity. A number of precious materials are described below. The Price entry for each material gives the Price of a simple non-magical item made of that material, based on its Bulk (if the item is lighter than 1 Bulk, use the price for 1 Bulk), as well as Prices for different amounts of the material itself. Costs for armor, shields, and weapons crafted from precious materials appear later in this chapter.

Bronzewood
Material 5+

UNCOMMON
precious
This exceptionally hard wood, native to Aerenal, is useful in the manufacture of armor and weapons. Unlike most woods, bronzewood can be used instead of metal to fashion heavy armor and weapons—it is somewhat shapeable during manufacturing, and it keeps a sharp edge. Although dense and weighty compared to other woods, it is still lighter than steel. A metal item made of bronzewood is lighter than one made of iron or steel: the item’s Bulk is reduced by 1 (with no effect on an item that normally has 1 Bulk or less). The Price of a metal item made of this material is based on the item’s normal Bulk, not its reduced Bulk for being made of bronzewood, but reduce the Bulk before making any further Bulk adjustments for the size of the item.

Densewood
Material 2+

precious
One of the most important products of the forests of Aerenal, densewood is a strong, hard, heavy wood similar to iron in its properties. Densewood is used in the construction of wood items and in the construction of shrines or buildings by Aereni and Valenar artisans. Items made from densewood are very durable and are also heavier than items made from other woods: the item’s Bulk is increased by 1 (with no effect on an item that normally has light Bulk or less). The Price of an item made of this material is based on the item’s normal Bulk, not its increased Bulk for being made of densewood, but increase the Bulk before making any further Bulk adjustments for the size of the item.

Flametouched iron
Material 8+

uncommon
precious
Mined only in Thrane, flametouched iron is rare and considered sacred by the Church of the Silver Flame. When mined, this iron variety has a speckled dark red color, resembling rust, but when it is refined, it takes on a shimmering, silvery hue. Adherents of the Church of the Silver Flame believe that flametouched iron carries the particular blessing of their deity, and they use it to make holy symbols, weapons, and armor.

Livewood
Material 14+

uncommon
precious
Another of the unusual woods of Aerenal, livewood is a green-colored hardwood with a highly magical nature. When livewood trees are felled, they do not die, though they stop all growth. Livewood can be worked like normal hardwood, while it remains completely alive. In most respects, livewood objects are living plants.

soarwood
Material 13+

uncommon
precious
Rare even in the abundant forests of Aerenal, soarwood possesses a magical buoyancy. Ships made from soarwood skim effortlessly over the surface of the water. Soarwood is a necessary component of the airships and elemental galleons manufactured by House Orien and House Cannith. Soarwood’s peculiar resistance to gravity makes it unsuitable for most forms of craftsmanship, as weapons and armor forged from it perform poorly and lose their effectiveness. A soarwood item’s Bulk is reduced by 3 (or to light Bulk at minimum, with no effect on an item that normally has light Bulk). The Price of an item made of soarwood is based on the item’s normal Bulk, not its reduced Bulk for being made of soarwood, but reduce the Bulk before making any further Bulk adjustments for the size of the item.

targath
Material 8+

uncommon

Targath is a soft metal mined on the northern coast of Argonnessen. Often fashioned in curative magic items, targath naturally possesses some of Irian's planar essence. Targath is, for reasons that are not clear, anathema to the deathless of Aerenal: They shrink from its touch, and a weapon fashioned from targath can overcome their resistance. Perhaps fortunately for the Undying Court, weapons made of targath are inferior to steel weapons.

Precious Materials Armor

Suits of armor made of precious materials are more expensive and sometimes grant special effects. You can make metal armor out of any precious materials shown above except for densewood, livewood and soarwood, or you can make wooden armor out of livewood. Because armor’s Bulk is reduced when the armor is worn, use its carried Bulk when determining its material Price.

bronzewood armor
Item 7+

UNCOMMON
worn armor varies by armor
The surface of bronzewood armor is dull yet elegant, its grain marked by intertwining veins of deep and bright bronze. A metal armor made of bronzewood is lighter than one made of iron or steel: the armor’s Bulk is reduced by 1 (with no effect on a armor that normally has 1 Bulk or less). It’s easier to wear than normal metal armor, reducing the Strength score necessary to ignore its check penalty by 2.
standard-grade bronzewood armor 7 800 gp + 80 gp per Bulk The initial raw materials must include bronzewood worth at least 100 gp + 10 gp per Bulk
high-grade bronzewood armor 12 16,000 gp + 1,600 gp per Bulk The initial raw materials must include bronzewood worth at least 8,000 gp + 800 gp per Bulk
