Pathfinder 2e Remastered Eberron

The features that most set Eberron apart are its tone and attitude. The setting combines traditional medieval fantasy with pulp action and dark adventure. Make no mistake—the world of Eberron proudly takes its place among the D&D worlds that have come before, with a cinematic flair and an eye toward the best action-adventure movies ever filmed. The campaign’s story elements were designed with this in mind.

The world of Eberron has a rich history built on heroic deeds, evolving magic, and the wounds of a long, devastating war. In the wake of this Last War, action, adventure, good, evil, and a thousand shades of gray paint the landscape in broad, powerful strokes, and ancient mysteries await discovery so that they too can influence the world and its people.

Magic is built into the very fabric of the setting. It pervades and influences everyday life. It provides certain comforts and conveniences unknown in either the modern world or any world of medieval fantasy. Great cities where castles scrape the sky can be found throughout the continent of Khorvaire, and a thriving aristocracy of merchant families controls much of the world’s economy thanks to the edge given them by the mysterious and rare dragonmarks.

Heroes come in all shapes and sizes, all classes and races. They travel the world, battling villains and recovering fabulous treasure, dealing with over-the-top action, harrowing challenges, cliffhanger situations, narrow escapes, and ominous mysteries that are as likely to shed light on centuries of secrets as they are to threaten the safety of the current day

Symbionts

Symbiots are a special kind of creature that straddles the line between disease and magical objects. They are complete creatures, generally capable of surviving apart from a host creature for at least some days. They are generally Tiny or smaller, and physically limited on their own. They survive by joining with a host creature, usually granting the host certain benefits in exchange for the protection afforded by a larger body. This symbiotic relationship usually benefits both parties (though for many the symbiont might better be described as a parasite).

Symbiots rules

A worn symbiont is treated as a magical object with the intelligent trait. Symbionts can’t be crafted by normal means. Because of this, symbionts are always rare. The normal statistics and rules for wearing or using an item of its type still apply to a symbiont. In addition, symbionts have statistics other items lack.

Attribute Modifiers

While grafted symbiots gain the physical attribute (Str, Dex, Con) of their host, but always keep their Int, Wis and Cha modifier.

Communication and Languages

Symbionts communicate by empathy and telepathy. Telepathy function as it does for any creature, while an empathic connection allows the symbiot to share only emotions. Empathic and telepathic connections are limited either by contact (0 ft) or to a very short distance. If a symbiont understands any languages, they are listed in its Communication entry. However, a host always understand its symbiot and vice-versa.

Perception and Senses

An symbiont that has any sense of the world around them has a Perception modifier. Symbionts have only the senses listed within their entry. A symbiont with a host loses its limited senses and perceives the world through its host's sense.

Save

Unattached symbiots have their listed saves. However, while hosted, the symbiot take the For and/or Ref saves of its host, whichever is higher.

Skills

Symbiots have skill modifiers that fit their nature. They are quite often skilled in intimidation and deception.

Speed and action

Unattached Symbiots can move and act normally. They have the listed speed and movement type. However, an attached symbiot can't normally act without the consent of its host. Then all symbiots activities use the host's actions for the turn.

Life

A symbiont is a living creature. Its hit points are equal to 3 to 5 times its item level. However, if the symbiont has a host, any damage to the symbiont will first deplete the host's hit points before affecting the symbiont's own.

Dealing damage to a symbiont attached to a dying host has the same effect as dealing damage directly to the host, increasing is dying condition value.

Symbiots Encounter

An encounter with a symbiot typically follows a predictable sequence. Initially, the prospective host is subjected to the symbiote’s empathic link, which is projected from a concealed position nearby. This link elicits heightened curiosity, prompting the host to approach the source. As proximity increases, the symbiote emits emotional stimuli characterized by sensations of affection and psychological comfort, encouraging physical contact. Upon tactile interaction, the symbiot may initiate a transient neural override, facilitating a brief period of behavioral influence intended to predispose the host to accept parasitization.

Additionally, symbiots are known to prefer approaching victims who are sleeping outdoors. Taking advantage of the stillness of the night, they move in silently and initiate contact with the sleeping host. This state of reduced consciousness facilitates impulse override, allowing the symbiot to more easily establish a communion with the host organism

Communion with a Symbiot

Communion with a symbiot can be initiated in one of two ways. Once physical contact is established, the symbiot may initiate telepathic communication—employing diplomacy, intimidation, or deception—to offer its services to the target. The victim, as a potential host, may then willingly choose to enter into communion with the symbiot.

Alternatively, in last resort, the symbiots can induce a state of temporary madness via telepathy, compelling the victim to surrender briefly to its influence. This short-lived but effective loss of control allows the symbiote to complete the communion without resistance.

Worn symbiot are considered invested item.

Living with a symbiots

Physiologically, the symbiote is fully integrated into the host’s metabolism. Nothing distinguishes the creature from its bearer—every wound, ailment, or affliction is borne entirely by the host. The symbiote’s flesh constantly releases a psychoactive substance into the host’s bloodstream, rendering the body more susceptible to the symbiote’s psychic influence.

Psychologically, life with a symbiote is marked by a constant and tense exchange with an internal voice—one noted for its cold detachment and unsettling cruelty. The host must learn to coexist with this strange presence, a voice heard by no one else. Furthermore, they must navigate the challenges of maintaining this uneasy alliance on a daily basis. This ongoing struggle for mental dominance, compounded by the effects of the psychoactive secretion, has been named by scholars: symbiosytis.

Symbiosytis

Parasitic symbiosis (or symbiosytis) is a near incurable disease of both body and mind, contracted by the host of a symbiote from the very first moment of their communion. The state of this infection is directly tied to the symbiote's mental hold over its host, which, in turn, shapes the relationship between the two entities.

Silence the voices

Ridding oneself of a symbiote is an extremely difficult task, requiring patience, strength of character, and deep conviction. Most known methods are harsh on both body and mind, and the majority leave the newly freed host with severe and permanent aftereffects.

Consentual disjunction

The most pleasant way to part with a symbiote is, of course, to convince it to end the communion on its own terms. That said, this kind of social intervention requires tremendous patience. A symbiote will rarely agree to leave behind its only way of life and the comfort of a host unless offered a better alternative—and, paradoxically, unless it is convinced to overcome its own fears. Such an effort can only truly begin, or have even the faintest chance of success, if the relationship between the host and the symbiote has lasted for several months, if not years.

If the host has managed to convince their symbiote of the fairness of separation, the symbiote can end the communion within seconds, leaving no lasting trace on the host—except for any permanent damage related to the symbiote’s nature. For instance, a lost eye or a lost tongue will not regrow.

Bringing up this option with a not friendly symbiote is a bad idea. Their jealous, cruel, and sadistic nature can easily turn against the host who dares to suggest separation too often. In fact, simply mentioning the idea to an antagonistic symbiot can aggravate its Symbiosytis stage.

Dismemberement

The easiest way to get rid of a symbiot is, of course, to simply tear it from its host. With brute force and the help of bladed or piercing objects, the symbiot can be forcibly extracted from its victim’s body. That said, this approach is difficult and dangerous—for both the host and those attempting the removal.

First of all, a symbiot is always aware of what its host knows. As such, it's nearly impossible to catch a symbiot by surprise. A host considering the amputaion will certainly aggravate the symbiosytis by more than one stage (at the GM's discretion). This means that during an extraction attempt, the symbiot will be fully conscious and, unsurprisingly, hostile toward the people trying to remove it. As a result, the patient and the extractor will have to face a constant barrage of temporary madness induced by the symbiot.

Fortunately, a symbiot is also asleep when its host is asleep. So, those preparing the procedure usually wait for the host to fall asleep—or be rendered unconscious by other means—before beginning.

Finally, The removal of a symbiot is an impossible and deadly procedure if not performed by someone at least trained in medicine.

Secret of the Gatekeeper

Finally, over the centuries, the Gatekeepers have developed a ritual that allows for a consensual disjunction between a symbiot and its host. The process is clearly easier and far more pleasant—but finding a group of Gatekeepers capable of performing is another story.
